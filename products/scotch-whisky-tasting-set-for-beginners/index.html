The Single Malts

Six diverse styles

Premium Aged

Ledaig 18

The Isle of Mull

Peated, yet rounded with big flavour and depth from the cask. Christmas spice, dried fruits and swirling smoke. Matured for at least 18 years in ex-sherry and ex-bourbon casks.

Vanilla Sweet

Auchentoshan American Oak

The Lowlands

Great for bourbon lovers, this release has been matured exclusively in American oak ex-bourbon, with vanilla and coconut at the forefront.

Soft Smoke

Glen Moray Peated

Speyside

A smooth spirit from the sweet Speyside distillery. Glen Moray's single malts are affordable, light and palatable. This expression has gentle peat, and is a great representation of how soft a peated whisky can be.

Grassy & Malty

Glenkinchie 12

Lowlands

Nutty, cereal tones and a thick mouthfeel. Glenkinchie has enormous stills and uses a cold run worm tub to cut down on copper contact and promote a meatier spirit.

Complex & Coastal

Oban 14

Highlands

Full bodied, rich and complex. Oban's worm tubs are run hot, increasing copper contact and moderating the meaty tones that are typical of a worm tub, making this a very well-balanced malt whisky.

Rugged Maritime

Talisker 10

Isle of Skye

Cracked black pepper and spice, rich oak, smoke and citrus. Complex, bold and one of the renowned classics. For those who like the slightly more fierce and spicy single malts.
