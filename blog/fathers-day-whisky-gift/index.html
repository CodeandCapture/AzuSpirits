The problem with buying whisky for a dad who already drinks it

The challenge with a whisky gift for dad is that an enthusiast is usually already buying exactly what he likes. If he has a favourite distillery, there's a reasonable chance he already owns at least one expression from it. Buy him the same bottle and it's a duplication. Buy him something unfamiliar and you risk the wrong fit.

A whisky tasting set solves this neatly. Instead of committing to one bottle, you're giving him six different premium expressions — presented blind, without the labels — so he tastes with genuine curiosity rather than expectation. He makes his own judgements first. Then comes the reveal.

"The blind format means even a dad with an impressive whisky cabinet will encounter bottles he genuinely hasn't tasted before — in a format that doesn't exist anywhere else."

Our tasting sets each include six premium single malts from renowned and lesser known distilleries, but always featuring single malts you're unlikely to find on the highstreet. Curated by whisky lovers, we select a diverse range of styles in each gift set that we know Scotch whisky lovers will adore.

Why choose a tasting set over a single bottle as a whisky gift for dad

There's a version of this question that sometimes comes up: isn't a great single bottle more satisfying than a set of samples? It's a fair point — and the answer depends on what you want the gift to be.

A bottle is something to enjoy. A tasting set is something to experience. There's anticipation in tasting blind, conversation in debating what each dram might be, and a genuine moment of discovery when the reveal envelope is opened. It's an event, not just a drink — offering a different kind of Father's Day gift.

With premium single malts selected by specialists, we make sure our tasting sets present six different distilleries, styles, and ages. Even an experienced whisky drinker rarely gets the chance to taste Scotland's six regions systematically side by side — or to encounter aged expressions like Glen Scotia 15 and Glenfiddich 15 Solera in the same sitting.
